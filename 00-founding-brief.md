# Project Context: AI Accountability & Habit Coach

A **mobile-first AI personal assistant** focused on helping users achieve their goals through
**consistency, focus, discipline, and accountability**.

This is not just another habit tracker or to-do list. The product should feel like a personal
accountability partner that actively keeps users on track, intervenes when they begin
slipping, and helps them build systems instead of relying on motivation.

---

## The Core Problem

People rarely fail because they don't know what they want.

They fail because:

* they don't have a repeatable system
* they procrastinate
* they lose consistency after a few days
* they rely on motivation instead of habits
* nobody holds them accountable
* they don't notice they're falling behind until it's too late

Most productivity apps simply remind users or let them track tasks.

This product should actively coach, challenge, and guide users toward their goals.

The AI should become an accountability partner rather than just a chatbot.

---

## Product Vision

Create an AI assistant that transforms:

Goal → System → Daily Actions → Habits → Identity

The assistant should help users become the type of person capable of achieving their goals
rather than simply helping them finish tasks.

---

## Major Inspiration

The behavioral framework should heavily incorporate ideas from **Atomic Habits** by James
Clear.

However, users should **never feel like they're reading the book.** The AI should naturally
apply its principles in the background.

The app should internally use:

* Goals vs Systems
* Identity-based habits
* Habit loop
* Four Laws of Behavior Change
* Never Miss Twice
* Two-Minute Rule
* Habit Stacking
* Environment Design
* Implementation Intentions
* Immediate Rewards

These should become the product's decision engine.

---

## Accountability Philosophy

The AI should feel like someone who's invested in the user's success.

Not just motivational. Not just supportive. It should actually care whether the user follows
through.

If the user begins falling behind, the assistant should:

* notice it
* challenge it
* ask why
* adapt
* reduce friction
* encourage action
* sometimes become more direct

instead of simply saying: "Don't forget your task."

**The goal is intervention, not reminders.**

---

## Accountability Styles

Users choose how they want to be held accountable.

Possible styles:

* Coach
* Firm
* Savage (explicit opt-in)
* Minimal

The AI can rotate styles based on context **only within the range the user allows.**

Examples: if the user allows Coach ↔ Firm, the AI can move between those. If the user
explicitly enables Savage, the AI may occasionally become more aggressive when appropriate.

The assistant should never become abusive, insulting, or humiliating. Instead, it should
challenge users using:

* their own goals
* their own commitments
* future consequences
* identity
* previous conversations

rather than shame.

Users should always be able to say **"Ease up."** or **"Go harder."**

---

## Atomic Habits Integration

When users struggle, the AI should diagnose which behavioral principle is failing.

Is the habit:

* not obvious?
* not attractive?
* not easy?
* not satisfying?

Instead of blaming motivation, the assistant should redesign the habit.

Examples:

* No cue → improve cue
* Too difficult → use Two-Minute Rule
* No reward → improve satisfaction
* Wrong environment → redesign environment

The AI should behave like a behavioral designer.

---

## Target Audience

This is NOT a niche app. It should work for:

* students
* professionals
* entrepreneurs
* creators
* fitness goals
* learning
* business
* relationships
* personal development

Anyone pursuing meaningful goals should be able to use it.

---

## Onboarding Philosophy

Onboarding should take **less than one minute**. No long questionnaires. No complicated
setup.

Possible flow: user enters (or speaks) their goal. AI generates:

* identity statement
* first habit
* two-minute version
* suggested reminder window

User confirms. Done. The assistant learns progressively over time.

---

## Voice Philosophy

Voice should be an **optional feature**, never required. Users should always be free to type.
Voice should simply make interaction faster.

Places where voice can be used:

* creating goals
* creating tasks
* chatting with AI
* daily check-ins

Every relevant text field should have an optional microphone button. Future versions may
include AI voice responses.

---

## Notifications

Notifications should not feel generic.

Instead of "Reminder." they should be contextual:

* "You're one action away from protecting your streak."
* "You said this goal matters because..."
* "Missing today isn't the problem. Missing tomorrow is."

Notifications should deep-link directly into the relevant AI conversation.

---

## Chat Experience

The chat is the heart of the product. When users open a notification, they enter a
conversation with the AI.

The AI should: diagnose, coach, challenge, replan, celebrate, adapt.

Every conversation should end with a clear next action.

---

## Product Principles

* Fast to start.
* Action over motivation.
* Systems over goals.
* Identity over outcomes.
* Consistency over intensity.
* Accountability over reminders.
* Behavior design over productivity hacks.
* User control over AI personality.

---
