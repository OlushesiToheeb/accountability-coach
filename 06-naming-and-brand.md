# 06 — Naming & Brand (working doc)

> **Status:** candidate stage. Twenty names below, filtered against the doc-01 criteria;
> founder to shortlist, then we run availability vetting (§4) before anything is final.
> Branding proper (§5) is the follow-up workstream once the name direction is set.

## 1. Criteria (from doc 01, expanded)

A good name here should:

1. **Imply a person who holds the line, not a tool.** We're naming a coach, not a tracker.
2. **Carry the thesis** — comeback, evidence, commitment, or steadiness — without being a
   habit cliché (no Habitly/Streakify/GoalPro).
3. **Be short and verb-able or idiom-backed.** "In your corner", "rally", "tally it up" —
   names that live inside existing speech get free marketing.
4. **Survive the notification test:** how does it feel when *this name* pings you at 8am?
   ("Rally: yesterday's gone. 2 minutes.") A name that sounds right in a lock-screen
   sender-line is doing its job.
5. **Not be fatally colonized** in the self-improvement/app space (checked properly in §4;
   first-pass notes below are from memory and need verification).

## 3. Founder-facing shortlist (my ranked five)

1. **Rally** — the only candidate that *is* the differentiated thesis (comeback over streak).
   Verb, noun, and rallying-cry at once; best notification voice of the twenty.
2. **Tally** — the ledger/votes mechanic as a name; warm where Rally is kinetic. (Rally vs.
   Tally is genuinely a brand-personality decision: fight vs. craft.)
3. **Corner** — the relationship promise ("in your corner"), already embedded in our own
   product language; supports the coach-character direction without requiring it.
4. **Pact** — the commitment contract, category-native, ten years of pent-up demand behind
   the dead predecessor; needs the trademark check most.
5. **Kept** — the dark horse: quietest, most distinctive, ships with its own tagline.

## 4. Vetting checklist (run on the shortlist before deciding)

- [ ] App Store + Play Store search: what already ranks for the name?
- [ ] Trademark search (USPTO + WIPO, classes 9 & 42 at minimum; NG/UK if relevant)
- [ ] Domain reality check: `get<name>.app`, `<name>.coach`, `join<name>.com` all acceptable
      patterns — exact-match .com is not required in 2026
- [ ] Handle check: TikTok/IG/X (the beachhead lives on self-improvement content channels)
- [ ] The notification test (§1.4) read aloud with real copy from doc 04 §7
- [ ] Say-it-on-a-podcast test: unambiguous spelling when heard, not seen

## 5. Branding workstream (queued — first fork to decide)

**The persona fork, before any visual identity:** is the coach a **named character**
(Duolingo's Duo model — the character *is* the notification voice, the memes, the moat) or
an **abstract brand presence** (the app speaks as itself)? This decision shapes the name
choice (Vera vs. Rally), the icon, every line of copy, and how Savage mode is perceived
(a character being savage reads as personality; an app being savage reads as policy).
Leaning at time of writing: *abstract name + strongly consistent voice*, revisit character
after Phase 0 shows how users talk about the coach ("it" vs. "he/she/they" in pilot
transcripts is the tell).

Then, in order: tone-of-voice guide (doc 03 §4 is the seed), icon + palette, store
listing/screenshots narrative (beachhead-tuned per D1), landing page.
